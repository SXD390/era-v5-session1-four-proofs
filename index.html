Depth without nonlinearity is a lie.

S1-3

Embeddings learn similarity from nothing but next-token.

Claim. Trained only to predict the next token in a tiny synthetic grammar, the embedding table clusters related words — even though no notion of similarity was ever supplied.
Idle — grammar: animal → verb → fruit.

Learned 2-D embedding space epoch 0

animals fruits verbs

What the model actually sees supervision

The vocabulary is 9 words in 3 categories:

animals = cat · dog · cow
fruits  = apple · mango · banana
verbs   = eat · chase · see

Sentences follow one rule — animal verb fruit ("dog chase mango") — so the only signal is which word tends to follow which. Words in the same category share the same next-token statistics.

No coordinates, no labels, no similarity is given. Yet minimizing next-token loss alone forces same-category words to the same place.
