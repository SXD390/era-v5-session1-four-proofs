S1-1

Activations exist for a reason.

A model with no activation can only draw a straight line, useless for two concentric rings. Add one nonlinear hidden layer and the boundary bends into a closed loop. The data and the training stay the same; only the activation changes.

S1-2

Depth without nonlinearity is a lie.

Five stacked linear layers collapse to one linear map, so a 5-layer linear net is no stronger than a single layer. Both fail the ring the same way. Insert ReLUs between the same five layers and it works. To see why, multiply the five trained weight matrices and watch them become one.

S1-3

Embeddings learn similarity from nothing but next-token.

A tiny model trains only to predict the next word in a toy grammar (animal → verb → fruit). You never tell it which words are alike, yet its 2-D embeddings settle into three clean clusters. Scrub the timeline to watch the clusters form.

Vocabulary: 9 words, 3 categories.

animals = cat · dog · cow
fruits  = apple · mango · banana
verbs   = eat · chase · see

Every sentence is animal verb fruit ("dog chase mango"). The only signal is what tends to follow what, and same-category words share that pattern.

No coordinates, no labels, no similarity. Minimizing next-token loss pulls same-category words together.

S1-4

Memorization vs generalization, and data closes the gap.

Train the same over-parameterized net (64×64) on 20, 200, then 2000 noisy points. At 20 it memorizes: train ≈ 100%, held-out poor. At 2000 the gap nearly vanishes. The data changed, not the model.
